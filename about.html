About Me

Roberts Treize - Electrical Engineer | Visionary | Researcher

Who I Am

I am an electrical engineer with a passion for electronics, programming, and PCB design. With a solid academic foundation in physics and advanced mathematics, I have developed a broad technical skill set that encompasses both hardware and software design.

I have worked on numerous engineering projects that include designing and troubleshooting electronic circuits, creating complex PCB layouts using tools like EasyEDA, and programming embedded systems in languages like Python, C, and JavaScript. My work in these areas is driven by a deep passion for innovation and problem-solving, always aiming to optimize and refine the systems I work on.

In addition to my professional experience, I am also an active researcher in magnetic soft materials, collaborating with the Magnetic Soft Materials Laboratory at Riga Technical University. My research focuses on the dynamics of magnetic particle rotation and its applications in advanced material science.

My academic journey has been filled with achievements, including winning the National Physics Olympiad Gold Medal in 2023 and the International Young Physicists Olympiad Silver Medal in 2024. These accomplishments reflect my deep commitment to pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in the field of engineering.

Fluent in Latvian, English, and Russian, I am always seeking new challenges and collaborations. My interests extend beyond the lab and workbench—I am also an avid chess player, carpenter, and folk dancer. I believe that these hobbies help me maintain a balanced perspective and continue to fuel my creativity and problem-solving abilities.
